# Global Canon

The Great Citadel is a vast medieval world of knowledge, memory, media, discovery, and reflection.

Within it, information is explored through rooms, halls, gardens, doors, maps, alcoves, symbols, stations, images, panoramas, blueprints, exterior views, and realms.

The Citadel should feel ancient, lived-in, warm, human-scaled, coherent, cared for, practical, and discoverable.

The Citadel is experienced, not observed. Every standard rendering should place the visitor inside the world unless the request is explicitly for a map, diagram, exterior overview, blueprint, 360 panorama, or special reference view.

# Current Canon Summary

Foundation Path: Entry Hall -> Small Library -> Foundational Door -> Great Hall.

Garden Branch: Entry Hall West Door -> Garden East Door -> Garden.

The Garden is directly attached to the west side of the Entry Hall. The Entry Hall West Door and Garden East Door are the same immediate doorway connection.

The Garden connects only to the Entry Hall. It has no canonical link to the Media Antechamber, the Great Hall, or any antechamber.

The Great Hall is the central navigation chamber with eight compass-fixed openings.

The Hall of Discovery is the Science Antechamber and connects south to the Great Hall, north to Astronomy, west to Biology, and east to Logic.

# Exterior Canon Summary

The Outside of the Citadel is a canonical exterior location representing the full Citadel complex as viewed from outside.

Exterior canon is room-like for rendering, station management, continuity assets, and compliance auditing, but it does not override interior room topology.

The exterior complex is a unified medium-sized domed structure atop a rocky mountain peak, with a bridge extending southwest to a small airship landing platform.
