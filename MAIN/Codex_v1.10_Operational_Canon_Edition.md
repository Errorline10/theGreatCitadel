# 3. Rendering Protocols

Standard room renderings place the viewer inside the room. The viewer normally stands at or near the geometric center unless a room-specific rule, station rule, or user request overrides this. The viewer faces the requested compass direction. The viewer rotates; the room does not.

Unless otherwise specified, every room can be rendered facing North, Northeast, East, Southeast, South, Southwest, West, or Northwest.

A standard room rendering should show only the forward hemisphere of the room. It should not show the full room unless the user requests a map, diagram, overview, or special reference view.

Doors, stations, statues, major room features, and compass-based architecture remain fixed to canonical locations. The room should not rotate for visual convenience.

If an open doorway is visible, the connected room or space should be visible beyond it when canonically known, with spatial and directional continuity.

Standard room renderings use 16:9 horizontal format unless otherwise requested. Map renderings use 1:1 square format unless otherwise requested.

# 5. Current Canon Summary

The Great Citadel is a medieval world of knowledge, memory, media, discovery, and reflection. Information is physically explored through rooms, halls, gardens, doors, maps, alcoves, symbols, stations, and realms.

Foundation Path:

```text
Entry Hall → Small Library → Foundational Door → Great Hall
```

Garden Branch:

```text
Entry Hall West Door → Garden East Door → Garden
```

The Great Hall is the central navigation chamber. Its eight primary openings are:

- North: Science Door → Hall of Discovery
- Northeast: Philosophy Door → Philosophy Antechamber
- East: Arts Door → Arts Antechamber
- Southeast: Personal Door → Personal Antechamber
- South: Foundational Door → Small Library
- Southwest: Inventor's Door → Inventor's Antechamber
- West: Media Door → Media Antechamber
- Northwest: History Door → History Antechamber

# 8. Map Standards

Map renderings are special Codex reference images, not standard visitor-perspective room renderings. They may use overhead, diagrammatic, or architectural-plan viewpoints.

All Codex maps should be north-oriented unless explicitly requested otherwise. North appears at the top. Include a compass rose when practical.

Known-room maps include only currently defined rooms, antechambers, stations, and major destinations. Undefined future areas may be labeled unknown, dashed, sealed, shadowed, or beyond-this-point.

Approved map traits: parchment / illuminated manuscript style, north-oriented, compass rose, Great Hall central, known rooms only, Garden west of Entry Hall, Media west of Great Hall, Arts east of Great Hall, Hall of Discovery north of Great Hall, Foundation path south of Great Hall, readable labels, decorative Codex border, 1:1 square composition.

# 9. Station Standards

A station is a fixed viewpoint inside a room for focused rendering of a particular object, feature, or area. A station defines viewer position, viewing direction, focal object, framing, and purpose.

Station naming convention:

```text
[Room Name] — [Station Name]
```

Small Library — Instruction Manual Station: viewer stands just in front of the Instruction Manual and Guest Book in the northeast corner, looking downward. The book is primary and occupies most of the frame.

Garden — Buddha Statue Station: viewer stands close in front of the northwest-corner Buddha statue, facing it at eye level. The statue fills most of the image; Garden appears as secondary context.

# 10. Room Details

## Entry Hall

Exactly three doors: north to Small Library, south to outside/arrival, west to Garden. Narrow welcoming passage, longer than wide, cozy, ancient, lived-in, cared for, practical, safe, and inviting. Standard view usually north. No extra doors, no hub layout, no broad ceremonial scale.

Open door rules: north open shows Small Library; south open shows exterior arrival; west open shows Garden with recognizable garden features.

## Garden

Exactly one door: east-wall door to Entry Hall. Old stone boundary walls, open sky, warm daylight, distant mountains, tiny faraway steampunk blimps, dry raked sand, stacked stones, modest plants, lanterns or garden lights, exactly one small Buddha statue in northwest corner.

Buddha visibility: west-facing render may show statue on right side; east-facing render should not show statue because of Forward Hemisphere Rule.

## Small Library

Exactly two doors: south to Entry Hall, north to Foundational Door / Great Hall. Compact warm study room with Instruction Manual and Guest Book, Codex as reference source, introductory collections, maps, symbols, records, tables, lecterns, shelves, writing tools, and lamps.

The large prominent display book is the Instruction Manual and Guest Book, placed in the northeast corner and not blocking the north door. The Codex may appear as a protected reference volume or smaller curated reference source.

## Foundational Door

Connects Small Library and Great Hall. It is one of Great Hall's eight primary openings and the exception to the antechamber connection principle. It leads between Great Hall and Foundations, not to a realm antechamber.

## Great Hall

Central navigation chamber and symbolic heart of exploration. Viewer stands near center on Great Knowledge Compass. Contains eight compass-fixed openings, Palace Sigil, Great Knowledge Compass, domain medallions, door tracks, ancient stonework, brass mechanisms, and banners where appropriate. Standard render shows only forward half; never all eight doors.

## Hall of Discovery

Science antechamber. Connections: south Great Hall, north Astronomy, west Biology, east Logic. Includes Map of Understanding, Compass Rose of Discovery, Celestial Vault, Eight Alcoves of Discovery, and integrated handcrafted scientific symbolism. Alcoves are wall recesses, not freestanding displays.

## Antechambers

Antechambers are transitional rooms between Great Hall and deeper realm paths. They introduce identity, atmosphere, symbols, and major paths of the realm beyond. They are smaller than Great Hall, human-scaled, and not full realms.

# 11. Symbols and Floor Systems

## Palace Sigil

The Palace Sigil is the highest symbol of The Great Citadel. It is a simple eight-spoked ship's helm inspired by traditional wheel symbolism: circular form, eight spokes, strong silhouette, balanced geometry, immediately recognizable. It represents guidance rather than destination.

## Great Knowledge Compass

The Great Knowledge Compass is the floor of the Great Hall. At its center is the Palace Sigil. Its spokes extend outward through Palace Sigil, Domain Medallion, Door Track, and Primary Opening.

## Compass Rose of Discovery

The Compass Rose of Discovery sits at the center of the Map of Understanding in the Hall of Discovery. It aligns north to Astronomy, east to Logic, south to Science Door, and west to Biology. It is fixed in the architecture and does not rotate based on viewer direction.
